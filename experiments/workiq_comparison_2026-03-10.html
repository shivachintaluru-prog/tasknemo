Why Approach A Missed 7 Tasks

The "Spotlight" Problem

A's meeting query analyzed 1 of 4 transcribed meetings (Voice notes team sync). It completely skipped UI Discussion, AI First PM Forum, and Copilot Insight Engine Office Hour — dropping 6 tasks silently.

No Outbound Tracking

A's unreplied-items query only caught inbound messages. It had no mechanism to find "things I sent that got no reply" — missing the Praveen Sinha follow-up entirely.

Wasted Query Slot

A1 (broad sweep: "What are my top action items?") returned zero data. 33% of A's query budget produced nothing. B had 2 empty queries too (doc mentions), but 8 others compensated.

No Completion Detection

A presented the Ajay demo request and Mohit rollout dates as fully open. B's sent-items-first approach detected these were already partially addressed, reducing false-positive noise.

Key Observations

1. No Hallucinations in Either Approach

Contrary to prior incidents (e.g., fabricated "Juhee All Hands speaker slot"), WorkIQ did not hallucinate in this window. The problem is omission, not fabrication. WorkIQ may have improved its grounding, or hallucination risk is context-dependent.

2. Accuracy Parity When Found

Both approaches scored identically on accuracy (2.0) and specificity (1.86) for tasks they did find. WorkIQ's interpretive summaries are well-grounded — the quality gap is purely about coverage breadth.

3. B's Traceability Gap Is Cosmetic

B scored lower on traceability (1.24 vs 2.00) because transcript items link to meeting recordings, not message permalinks. In practice, both approaches point to the same meeting — the difference is presentation, not substance.

4. Cost-Quality Tradeoff Is Favorable

B uses 3.3x more queries for 1.5x more tasks + completion detection. The marginal cost (~7 extra API calls, ~30s) is negligible vs. the value of catching 7 additional real tasks.

Verdict: Raw + Extract Is the Right Architecture

Approach B found 100% of ground truth tasks vs. A's 67%. The key insight: interpretive queries silently drop coverage — analyzing 1 of 4 transcripts, missing outbound tracking, and returning empty on broad questions. TaskNemo's design choice to pull raw data and reason locally is validated.
